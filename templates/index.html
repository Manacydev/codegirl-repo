Safety Measures

  • Check the Domain: Always double-check the address bar. Phishing sites often use misspelled domains (e.g., G00gle.com instead of Google.com).
  • Look for HTTPS: Legitimate websites use a secure connection. Look for `https://` and a padlock icon 🔒 in the address bar.
  • Hover, Don't Click: Before clicking a link, hover your mouse over it to see the actual destination URL at the bottom of your browser.
  • Beware of Urgency: Attackers create a false sense of urgency to trick you into acting without thinking. Be wary of "immediate action required" messages.
  • Protect Personal Info: Never enter sensitive information like passwords or credit card numbers unless you are absolutely sure the site is legitimate.
